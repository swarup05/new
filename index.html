Waste Management Education

Recycling Guidelines

Learn how to properly recycle different materials to maximize efficiency and minimize contamination.

  • Plastics: Check the resin code (1-7) and follow local guidelines
  • Paper: Remove any non-paper components like plastic windows
  • Glass: Separate by color and remove lids
  • Metals: Rinse containers and flatten if possible

Composting at Home

Turn your organic waste into nutrient-rich compost for your garden.

  • Greens: Vegetable scraps, fruit peels, coffee grounds
  • Browns: Dry leaves, twigs, paper, cardboard
  • Avoid: Meat, dairy, oily foods, pet waste

Waste Reduction Tips

Reduce your waste generation with these simple strategies.

  • Use reusable bags, bottles, and containers
  • Buy in bulk to reduce packaging
  • Choose products with minimal packaging
  • Repair items instead of replacing them

Hazardous Waste Disposal

Proper handling of hazardous materials is crucial for safety and environmental protection.

  • Never pour chemicals down drains or toilets
  • Store in original containers with labels
  • Take to designated collection facilities
  • Includes: batteries, paints, cleaners, pesticides, electronics
